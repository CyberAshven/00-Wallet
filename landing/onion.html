How Onion Payments Work
Stealth HTLCs
Funds are locked in a Hashed Time-Locked Contract (HTLC) on BCH. The recipient claims with a preimage; if unclaimed, the sender refunds after a timelock. Each payment uses a one-time stealth address derived via ECDH — the real recipient never appears on-chain.
Onion Routing
Payments can hop through relay nodes. Each relay peels one encryption layer, forwards the HTLC to the next hop, and earns a fee. No relay sees the full route — only its immediate neighbors.
Nostr Coordination
Relay discovery, onion blobs, and settlement notifications travel through Nostr relays as encrypted events. No server, no account — just encrypted P2P signaling over a decentralized relay mesh.
